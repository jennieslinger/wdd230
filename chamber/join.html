Membership Levels

Bronze Membership

Cost:

This level is usually the most affordable option, with an annual fee ranging from $250 to $500.

Benefits:

  • Basic listing in the Chamber's online directory.
  • Access to Chamber events at a discounted rate.
  • Networking opportunities with other members.
  • Subscription to Chamber newsletters and updates.
  • Opportunity to participate in Chamber committees or task forces.

Silver Membership

Cost:

The annual fee for a silver membership generally ranges from $500 to $1,000.

Benefits:

  • Enhanced listing in the Chamber's online directory with a logo and additional information about the organization.
  • Increased visibility through sponsorship opportunities at Chamber events.
  • Access to educational seminars or workshops at no extra cost.
  • Invitations to exclusive networking events or luncheons.
  • Priority consideration for speaking opportunities at Chamber functions.
  • Recognition as a silver-level member in Chamber publications and communications.

Gold Membership

Cost:

Gold membership is the highest tier and typically ranges from $1,000 to $2,500 annually.

Benefits:

  • Premium placement in the Chamber's online directory with a featured listing.
  • Exclusive sponsorship opportunities for major Chamber events or initiatives.
  • VIP access to Chamber events, including reserved seating and special receptions.
  • Access to customized business development support or consulting services offered by the Chamber.
  • Complimentary tickets to Chamber galas or other signature events.
  • Priority referral services and introductions to key stakeholders or potential partners.
  • Prominent recognition as a gold-level member in all Chamber communications, including press releases and social media.

Non-Profit Membership

These benefits are indicative and may vary depending on the specific Chamber of Commerce. Non-profit organizations can choose the membership level that best aligns with their budget and strategic objectives. It's essential for organizations to consider their networking needs, visibility goals, and capacity to take advantage of the resources offered at each level when deciding on their membership tier.
